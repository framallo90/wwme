# Professional Sources Used

## 1) Purdue OWL - The Writing Process
- URL: https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/index.html
- Why it matters:
  - Supports a process-oriented model: planning, drafting, revising as iterative stages.
  - Justifies the pipeline structure used in this skill.

## 2) Purdue OWL - Revising Drafts
- URL: https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/revising_drafts/index.html
- Why it matters:
  - Reinforces revision as substantive rework, not just typo cleanup.
  - Informs the continuity and structural quality gates in this skill.

## 3) Purdue OWL - Basic Fiction Writing
- URL: https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/subject_specific_writing/creative_writing/basic_fiction_writing/index.html
- Why it matters:
  - Provides foundation for fiction components: character, plot, setting, POV.
  - Supports scene-level requirements tied to conflict and narrative movement.

## 4) UNC Writing Center - Revising Drafts
- URL: https://writingcenter.unc.edu/tips-and-tools/revising-drafts/
- Why it matters:
  - Reinforces macro-to-micro revision order.
  - Supports requiring explicit intent before line-level polishing.
